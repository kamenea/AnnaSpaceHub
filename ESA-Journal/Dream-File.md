# The Dream File - by Anna

---

> "Journey into the unknown, but for the first time in a long time, I feel excited."

This file holds the bigger picture — the hopes, missions, and micro-visions that keep me moving forward on days when progress feels slow. It's not a plan. It's a compass.

---

## The Big Dream

To contribute to a European Space Agency mission — whether that means writing software that processes satellite telemetry, designing autonomous navigation logic for a planetary rover, or building tools that help scientists interpret Earth observation data.

I want to work on something that matters beyond a screen. Space exploration does that.

---

## Missions That Inspire Me

- **Copernicus Programme** — Using satellites to monitor climate change, deforestation, and ocean health. This is AI and Earth observation at their most impactful.
- **ExoMars** — A rover on Mars, searching for signs of life. The autonomy challenges alone are extraordinary.
- **Galileo** — Europe's own GPS. Precision, reliability, and the quiet infrastructure of modern navigation.
- **Hera** — ESA's planetary defence mission. The idea that software could one day help protect Earth from an asteroid feels unreal in the best way.

---

## Micro-Visions

These are smaller, closer dreams — stepping stones toward the bigger one:

- **Write Python code that processes real Copernicus satellite data.** Not a tutorial. Real data, real results.
- **Simulate a rover navigating a crater.** Even a simple grid-based pathfinder feels like a rehearsal for something larger.
- **Understand ECSS software standards well enough to apply them.** Mission-grade thinking, not just side-project habits.
- **Attend an ESA open day or networking event in person.** Put a face to the ambition.
- **Land a role — any role — adjacent to space systems.** A foothold. That's all it takes.

---

## What I Bring

I'm not starting from zero. I bring:

- Years of software experience in high-stakes fintech environments, where correctness and reliability are non-negotiable.
- A genuine curiosity about AI and how it can be applied to sensing, autonomy, and decision-making.
- A designer's instinct — for clarity, for user needs, for how systems communicate.
- The discipline to document, reflect, and keep going when progress is invisible.
